Ghanesh Narasimhan

// Assistant Professor — Department of Mechanical Engineering

Dr. Narasimhan's research sits at the intersection of fluid dynamics, large-scale simulation, and data-driven modeling. He earned his PhD from Johns Hopkins University, where he worked on turbulence modeling for wind energy, transition phenomena, and magnetohydrodynamics.

At the Intelligent Turbulence Lab, the group develops novel computational frameworks — combining high-fidelity Large Eddy Simulation with machine learning classifiers and physics-informed models — to understand and predict turbulent flows in engineering and geophysical contexts.

The lab's work spans atmospheric boundary layer flows for wind farm optimization, bypass and subcritical transition to turbulence, wake interaction modeling for yawed turbines, and magnetohydrodynamic flows relevant to Earth sciences.

// Research Areas

What We Study

01 ——

🌀

Atmospheric Turbulence & Wind Energy

High-fidelity LES of wind turbine wakes in atmospheric boundary layers, including yaw-based control strategies, wind veer effects, and reduced-order wake models for farm-scale optimization.

02 ——

⚡

Transition to Turbulence

Capturing bypass and subcritical orderly transition using wall-modeled LES combined with ML classifiers (Self-Organizing Maps) to distinguish laminar and turbulent flow regions at reduced computational cost.

03 ——

🤖

ML-Augmented Turbulence Modeling

Developing data-driven closures and classifiers that augment physics-based simulations, enabling smarter sub-grid scale models and more accurate prediction of complex turbulent phenomena.

04 ——

🌍

Magnetohydrodynamics

MHD flow modeling for Earth science applications, examining how electromagnetic effects couple with turbulent fluid dynamics in geophysical contexts such as the Earth's outer core.

05 ——

💨

Wake Interactions & Control

Understanding counter-rotating vortex pair structures behind yawed turbines, wake deflection, and superposition effects in wind farms — toward intelligent real-time control of large-scale arrays.

06 ——

🔬

High-Performance Computing

Developing scalable numerical methods and HPC pipelines for large-scale turbulence simulations, leveraging GPU acceleration and modern parallel architectures for next-generation CFD.

// Selected Publications

Research Output

2022

Physical Review Fluids

Effects of wind veer on a yawed wind turbine wake in atmospheric boundary layer flow

Ghanesh Narasimhan, Dennice F. Gayme, and Charles Meneveau

LES study of wind veer effects on yawed wind turbine wakes in ABL flow. A veer correction term is derived for analytical wake models, and counter-rotating vortex pair structures are recovered by subtracting background veer vorticity.

2021

Physical Review Fluids

Large eddy simulation of transitional channel flow using a machine learning classifier to distinguish laminar and turbulent regions

Ghanesh Narasimhan, Charles Meneveau, and Tamer A. Zaki

Wall-modeled LES of bypass and subcritical orderly transition, using Self-Organizing Maps (SOM) to identify turbulent–non-turbulent interfaces and selectively apply wall modeling, successfully capturing friction Reynolds number evolution at DNS-level accuracy with significantly reduced cost.
